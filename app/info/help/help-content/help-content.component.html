Introduction

DSpace 8 provides powerful search capabilities to help you discover and access digital content efficiently. This guide covers all search features and filters available in DSpace 8, complete with examples to help you craft effective searches.

Note: DSpace 8 uses Apache Solr as its search engine, offering fast and flexible search functionality with advanced features like faceted searching, real-time indexing, and robust text analysis.

Basic Search

The basic search in DSpace 8 allows you to quickly search across all metadata fields. The search box is typically located at the top of the DSpace interface.

Basic Search Examples:

  • climate change - Finds items containing both "climate" and "change" in any metadata field
  • neural networks - Searches for items about neural networks
  • COVID-19 vaccine - Retrieves items related to COVID-19 vaccines

Tip: By default, basic search looks across all searchable fields including title, author, abstract, keywords, and full text (if available).

Advanced Search

Advanced search provides more control over your search by allowing you to specify fields, combine search terms with different operators, and apply multiple filters simultaneously.

Advanced Search Form Example:

Advanced Search Examples:

  • Title contains "quantum computing" AND Author equals "Nielsen, Michael" - Finds items with "quantum computing" in the title authored by Michael Nielsen
  • Abstract contains "climate change" AND Subject contains "policy" AND Date issued is after "2020" - Retrieves recent climate change policy papers
  • Title starts with "Artificial" AND Subject contains "ethics" NOT Author contains "Smith" - Finds items about artificial intelligence ethics not authored by anyone named Smith

Search Operators

DSpace 8 supports various operators to refine your search queries.

Operator Function Example
AND Finds items containing both terms blockchain AND finance
OR Finds items containing either term covid OR coronavirus
NOT Excludes items containing the specified term climate NOT policy
+ Term must be present +quantum computing
- Term must not be present artificial intelligence -ethics
" " Searches for an exact phrase "machine learning"
* Wildcard for multiple characters neuro*
? Wildcard for a single character wom?n
~n Fuzzy search with similarity (n is 0-2) algorithm~1
~n Proximity search for phrases (n is number of words) "climate change"~3

Search Operator Examples:

  • machine AND learning NOT supervised - Finds items about machine learning excluding those focused on supervised learning
  • "big data" OR "data mining" - Retrieves items containing either phrase
  • cell~ biology - Performs a fuzzy search for "cell" (matching "cells", "sell", etc.) along with "biology"
  • "renewable energy"~5 - Finds items where "renewable" and "energy" appear within 5 words of each other

Search Filters

DSpace 8 offers various filters to narrow down search results based on specific criteria.

Date Filters

Filter results by publication or submission date.

Date Filter Examples:

  • Date range: 2020-01-01 to 2023-12-31 - Limits results to items published between 2020 and 2023
  • Last 5 years - Shows only items from the past five years
  • Before 2010 - Displays items published before 2010
  • 2022 - Retrieves items from just the year 2022

Tip: You can also use date operators in advanced search with dateIssued:[2018-01-01 TO 2020-12-31] for more precise control.

Author Filters

Narrow results by author or contributor.

Author Filter Examples:

  • Smith, John - Shows only items authored by John Smith
  • Department of Physics - Filters results to items from the Physics Department
  • Multiple selection: Smith, John + Johnson, Mary - Retrieves items authored by either John Smith or Mary Johnson
  • ORCID: 0000-0002-1825-0097 - Finds items by the author with this ORCID identifier

Subject Filters

Filter results by subject keywords or classifications.

Subject Filter Examples:

  • Artificial Intelligence - Shows items tagged with this subject
  • Multiple selection: Climate Change + Environmental Policy - Finds items tagged with either subject
  • Computer Science > Machine Learning - Retrieves items within this hierarchical subject classification
  • COVID-19 - Displays items tagged with this specific subject

Collection Filters

Limit results to specific collections or communities.

Collection Filter Examples:

  • Research Articles - Shows only items from the Research Articles collection
  • Faculty of Science - Retrieves items from all collections within the Faculty of Science community
  • Conference Proceedings - Limits results to items in the Conference Proceedings collection
  • Multiple selection: Theses + Dissertations - Shows items from either collection

Document Type Filters

Filter results by the type of resource.

Document Type Filter Examples:

  • Article - Shows only journal articles
  • Thesis - Retrieves only theses
  • Conference Paper - Limits results to conference papers
  • Multiple selection: Book + Book Chapter - Shows either books or book chapters
  • Dataset - Retrieves only research datasets

Access Rights Filters

Filter results based on access permissions.

Access Rights Filter Examples:

  • Open Access - Shows only items with unrestricted access
  • Restricted - Retrieves items with limited access
  • Embargoed - Shows items currently under embargo
  • Metadata Only - Displays items where only metadata is accessible

Sorting Results

DSpace 8 allows you to sort search results based on various criteria.

Sorting Options:

  • Relevance - Default sort, items most closely matching your search query appear first
  • Title (A-Z/Z-A) - Alphabetical sorting by title
  • Date (newest/oldest) - Chronological sorting by publication date
  • Author (A-Z/Z-A) - Alphabetical sorting by first author
  • Issue Date (newest/oldest) - Sort by when item was issued
  • Most downloads - Sort by popularity based on download count

Tip: When looking for the most recent research, sort by "Date (newest first)" to see the latest publications at the top.

Boolean Operators

DSpace 8 supports Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT) to create complex search queries.

Boolean Operator Examples:

  • (climate AND change) OR (global AND warming) - Finds items about either climate change or global warming
  • cancer AND (treatment OR therapy) NOT surgery - Retrieves items about cancer treatments or therapies excluding surgical approaches
  • renewable AND (solar OR wind OR hydro) NOT nuclear - Finds items about renewable energy sources excluding nuclear
  • (COVID-19 OR coronavirus) AND (vaccine AND effectiveness) - Retrieves items about COVID-19 vaccine effectiveness

Note: Boolean operators must be capitalized (AND, OR, NOT) to be recognized as operators. When not capitalized, they are treated as regular search terms.

Phrase Searching

Use quotation marks to search for exact phrases in DSpace 8.

Phrase Search Examples:

  • "artificial intelligence" - Finds items containing this exact phrase
  • "climate change" AND "public policy" - Retrieves items containing both phrases
  • "quantum computing" NOT "quantum mechanics" - Finds items about quantum computing that don't mention quantum mechanics
  • "Internet of Things" AND "security challenges" - Retrieves items containing both phrases

Tip: For phrases with common words, using quotation marks ensures more relevant results by searching for the exact phrase rather than individual terms.

Wildcards and Truncation

DSpace 8 supports wildcards for more flexible searching.

Wildcard Examples:

  • environ* - Matches environment, environmental, environments, etc.
  • wom?n - Matches woman and women
  • colo*r - Matches color, colour, colorimeter, etc.
  • econom* - Matches economy, economic, economics, economical, etc.
  • neuro???ence - Matches neuroscience with any three characters in the middle

Note: The asterisk (*) replaces zero or more characters, while the question mark (?) replaces exactly one character.

Field-Specific Searching

DSpace 8 allows you to search specific metadata fields using field prefixes.

Field Search Examples:

  • title:"deep learning" - Searches only in the title field for the phrase "deep learning"
  • author:Smith AND subject:physics - Finds items authored by Smith in the field of physics
  • abstract:climate AND dateIssued:[2020 TO 2023] - Retrieves items with "climate" in the abstract published between 2020 and 2023
  • publisher:Elsevier AND title:neural - Finds items published by Elsevier with "neural" in the title
  • subject:"machine learning" AND author:Johnson - Retrieves machine learning papers by author Johnson
Common Field Prefixes Description
title: Searches in item titles
author: Searches for specific authors
subject: Searches in subject keywords
abstract: Searches in item abstracts
dateIssued: Searches by publication date
publisher: Searches by publisher name
language: Searches by content language
identifier: Searches for specific identifiers (DOI, ISBN, etc.)

Facet Navigation

DSpace 8 provides faceted navigation to refine search results dynamically.

Facet Navigation Example:

After searching for "climate change", the facet navigation panel might show:

  • Author facet:
    • Smith, John (42)
    • Johnson, Sarah (28)
    • Williams, David (17)
  • Subject facet:
    • Environmental Science (63)
    • Climate Policy (35)
    • Global Warming (29)
  • Date facet:
    • 2020-2023 (87)
    • 2015-2019 (64)
    • 2010-2014 (31)

Clicking on "Smith, John" under Author would refine results to show only climate change items by John Smith.

Tip: You can combine multiple facets (e.g., select both an author and a date range) to narrow down results more precisely.

Saving Searches

DSpace 8 allows registered users to save searches for future use.

How to Save a Search:

  1. Perform your search and apply desired filters
  2. Click the "Save this search" button (usually represented by a bookmark or star icon)
  3. Enter a name for your saved search
  4. Choose whether to receive email alerts for new matching items (optional)
  5. Click "Save"

Saved Search Examples:

  • "Recent AI Ethics Papers" - Saved search for "ethics" AND "artificial intelligence" with date filter for last 2 years
  • "My Department's Publications" - Saved search filtering by a specific department and recent years
  • "COVID Vaccine Research" - Saved search for COVID-19 vaccine research with alerts for new content

Search Tips and Best Practices

General Search Tips:

  • Start broad, then narrow: Begin with broader search terms, then use filters to refine results
  • Use synonyms: Try different terms for the same concept (e.g., "global warming" OR "climate change")
  • Check spelling: DSpace 8 has limited spelling correction, so verify your spelling
  • Use specific fields: Search specific fields (title:, author:) for more precise results
  • Combine approaches: Use both filters and Boolean operators for complex searches
  • Leverage facets: After an initial search, use facets to drill down into results
  • Utilize phrase searching: Put multi-word concepts in quotation marks
  • Save successful searches: When you find an effective search strategy, save it for future use

Search Strategy Examples:

  • For a literature review: Start with broader topic terms, then use facets to focus on recent years, specific authors, or methodologies
  • For finding specific research: Use field-specific searching with author names and keywords in the title or abstract
  • For interdisciplinary topics: Use OR between related discipline terms (e.g., "AI" OR "artificial intelligence" AND "ethics" OR "moral")
  • For comprehensive searches: Use wildcards and synonyms to capture variations (e.g., econom* AND (develop* OR grow*))
